Altered immune system

In the closed environment of a spacecraft, microorganisms are transferred easily increasing allergies and illnesses and the immune system is changed.

Celestial Dust Exposure

During surface exploration, extraterrestrial dust may have very harmful effects.

Temperature changes

It's really cold in space as there are no heat sources and it's farther from the sun.

Exposure to contaminants

In a spacecraft, some pollutants might be generated that will result in some undesirable effects.

Degenerative diseases

Radiation results in very unfortunate diseases, which depend on both the amount of radiation and the period of exposure.

Radiation sickness: Cancer

Exposure to radiation can lead to a type of cancer that has no available treatment or understanding.

Changes in central nervous system

The central nervous system (CNS) may be altered by the galactic cosmic rays, which may lead to a disease such as Alzheimer's disease.

Behavioral changes

These are the results of loneliness, like tension, and anxiety.

Sleep problems

Isolation affects the sleep-wake cycle badly.

Fatigue

People facing isolation feel tired and exhausted.

Decline in mode

Isolation results in depression and elevates stress hormone.

Ineffective medications

On a distant planet, medications won't be effective.

Food storage challenges

There'd be challenges dealing with how to keep food edible.

Lack of medical Care

Far from Earth, there wouldn't be any rapid medical help.

Equipment Failure

IF a device was inefficient after launching, no help will reach the astronaut.

Reduced Muscle Mass

In microgravity, muscle strength and endurance are reduced.

Bone loss

Weight-bearing bones lose some of their mineral density, in microgravity.

Fluid Shift

Shifting from a no-gravity condition to microgravity, results in fluids moving up in the body.

Changes in sensorimotor skills

Sensorimotor skills like balance and touching, are essential, but affected in microgravity.

Why scientists sent fruit flies into space?

For research here on Earth, the fruit fly is considered a model system to study because of its biological similarities to humans. Furthermore, due in part to this similarity, the fruit fly is an ideal subject for study in space.

What risks do fruitflies deal with?

They deal with

1. Diseases (immune system)
